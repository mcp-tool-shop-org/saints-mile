A frontier JRPG for the adults who grew up on 90s classics.

You play as Galen Rook across four decades — from a nineteen-year-old deputy's runner to an older man returning to the place where his life was written wrong. Turn-based party combat with standoff-driven initiative, a six-member roster with distinct battlefield roles, and a western road-story where the same skills mean different things when the world changes around them.

Built in Rust for the terminal. No graphics bloat. Full focus on party mechanics, branching consequence, and a story that trusts its audience.

### What kind of game is this?

- A 90s-style JRPG with a 4-slot party, distinct roles, duo techniques, and standoff-driven combat
- A frontier western where reputation is a web, distance changes decisions, and the trail is the dungeon
- A game for adults — themes of regret, duty, compromise, aging, loyalty, and starting over
- A terminal-native experience: runs in any terminal, any OS

### The story

You start wanted. Then the game rewinds to show you why.

From Cedar Wake's warmth to Bitter Cut's crack to the Saint's Mile relay where the poster is born — every chapter changes what your skills mean, who your party trusts, and what kind of truth you're carrying.

Six party members. Five truth types. Three relay branches. Four ending axes. One bell that never gets explained.

### What makes it different

The command menu carries biography. Young Galen fights with speed. Adult Galen fights with setup and command. Older Galen fights with judgment — because his hand was wounded and Quick Draw is gone, but Steady Aim survives because the gift outlives the giver.

### Features

- 16 chapters across four life phases
- 6 party members with distinct combat identities
- Standoff-driven initiative before every significant fight
- Nerve as a second health bar — panic breaks combatants on both sides
- Duo techniques unlocked through bonds and shared ordeals
- Crowd pressure, public reckoning, and split-party operations
- Three relay branches that change the evidence available for the rest of the game
- Four ending axes — justice, exposure, peace, or burden
- No procgen — every encounter is authored
- 151 tests. The game works.

### Content warnings

Violence (turn-based, consequential). Moral complexity (no clean choices). Adult themes (regret, institutional corruption, the cost of truth). Loss (permanent wounds, party dispersal). Occasional strong language.

Not present: sexual content, gore, horror, supernatural confirmation.

### Controls

- Arrow keys / WASD: navigate
- Enter / Space: confirm, advance text
- Escape: back
- Ctrl+S: quick save
- Ctrl+Q: quit

### How to run

Download the .exe and run it in any terminal (Windows Terminal, PowerShell, cmd). The game runs in your terminal — no installer, no dependencies, no internet required.

For Linux/Mac: clone the repo and `cargo run --release`.
