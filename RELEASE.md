# Saint's Mile — Release Notes

## One-Paragraph Pitch

A frontier JRPG for the adults who grew up on 90s classics. You play as Galen Rook across four decades — from a nineteen-year-old deputy's runner to an older man returning to the place where his life was written wrong. Turn-based party combat with standoff-driven initiative, a six-member roster with distinct battlefield roles, and a western road-story where the same skills mean different things when the world changes around them. Built in Rust for the terminal. No graphics bloat. Full focus on party mechanics, branching consequence, and a story that trusts its audience.

## Store Description

Saint's Mile is a frontier party RPG set in the Cinder Basin — a territory being reshaped by rail, water, and law. The game spans almost four decades of one man's life, told through turn-based combat, campfire conversations, and choices that change who trusts you, who remembers you, and what version of the truth survives.

**What kind of game is this?**

- A 90s-style JRPG with a 4-slot party, distinct roles, duo techniques, and standoff-driven combat
- A frontier western where reputation is a web, distance changes decisions, and the trail is the dungeon
- A game for adults — themes of regret, duty, compromise, aging, loyalty, and starting over
- A terminal-native experience that runs anywhere

**The story**

You start wanted. Then the game rewinds to show you why. From Cedar Wake's warmth to Bitter Cut's crack to the Saint's Mile relay where the poster is born — every chapter changes what your skills mean, who your party trusts, and what kind of truth you're carrying.

Six party members. Five truth types. Three relay branches. Four ending axes. One bell that never gets explained.

**What makes it different**

The command menu carries biography. Young Galen fights with speed. Adult Galen fights with setup and command. Older Galen fights with judgment — because his hand was wounded and Quick Draw is gone, but Steady Aim survives because the gift outlives the giver.

The same skills you loved using at the bandit camp feel different at Bitter Cut. The same engine that made the convoy feel alive makes the relay break hurt. The game trusts you to notice.

## Features

- **16 chapters** across four life phases (youth, young man, adult, older)
- **6 party members** with distinct combat identities and skill lines
- **Standoff-driven initiative** — posture, focus, nerve pressure before every significant fight
- **Nerve as second health bar** — panic breaks combatants on both sides
- **Duo techniques** — party synergy unlocked through bonds and shared ordeals
- **Crowd pressure system** — non-lethal civic combat where containment is victory
- **Environmental combat** — fuse charges, destructible cover, chain reactions
- **Public Reckoning** — five-bar pressure model for courtroom-as-battlefield
- **Split-party operations** — assignment as moral argument
- **Three relay branches** — one choice in Chapter 2 changes the evidence available for the rest of the game
- **Four ending axes** — justice, exposure, peace, or burden
- **Biography in the menu** — the command set changes by age, not just by level
- **Wound persistence** — injuries carry between encounters
- **Memory objects** — biscuit cloth, flask, poster, bell — echoing across chapters
- **No procgen** — every encounter is authored
- **Deterministic by design** — savable, replayable, debuggable

## Content Warnings

Saint's Mile contains:

- **Violence:** Turn-based frontier combat. Gunfights, suppression of laborers, relay ambush, civilian casualties. Violence is consequential, not gratuitous.
- **Moral complexity:** The player is placed in situations where no choice is clean. Complicity through obedience. Framing. Triage choices where someone dies regardless.
- **Adult themes:** Regret, aging, institutional corruption, historical fraud, the cost of truth. Adult language is present but restrained — swearing is occasional and earned.
- **Loss:** Party members are wounded, separated, and changed by the story. The hand injury is permanent. The dispersal is real.

**Not present:** Sexual content, gratuitous gore, horror elements, supernatural confirmation or denial (the bell stays ambiguous).

## Controls

- Arrow keys: navigate menus and choices
- Enter: confirm selection
- Escape/Q: back / quit
- Save/Load: accessible from any non-combat scene
